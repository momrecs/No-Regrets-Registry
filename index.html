The biggest decision

Strollers: You Need Two. I'm Sorry.

One compact stroller that lives in the car and folds in one motion, and one everyday stroller that can actually handle your climate. Tap your situation below and I'll tell you exactly what to get.

Yes, two strollers. No, this is not an excess thing. A travel stroller that can't handle snow and an everyday stroller that won't fit in your trunk are both useless half the year.

Non-negotiable safety gear

Car Seats: One for Each Stage

Three seats, three stages. Buy new, check expiry dates, and get the install checked. This is the one category where you don't cheap out or buy used.

Stage 1 · Bucket seat

Nuna Pipa

Light, safe, and clicks in and out without a wrestling match. Worth it for the newborn car-nap transfer alone.

Stage 2 · Convertible

Evenflo Revolve360

The seat rotates to face the door. Your back will thank you every single day. Once you've loaded a toddler into a rotating seat you cannot go back.

Stage 3 · Harness / booster

Graco Tranzitions

Grows from 5-point harness to booster. Simple, slim, does the job for years.

Everything here was tested across two babies and several hundred middle-of-the-night wakeups.

For the first months when baby sleeps in your room.

Crib

Whatever you like the look of. Truly.

Cribs all meet the same safety standard. Pick the pretty one, spend the savings elsewhere.

Crib mattress

A breathable one

This is where the crib money goes instead.

Travel crib

BabyBjörn

Light enough to carry one-handed, sets up in seconds. Once your kid is over 12 months, add a memory foam insert. Before one year, babies need the firm flat surface for safe sleep. And always pack an extra sheet when traveling.

Swaddle

The Ollie

Nothing better. Don't overthink this one.

Sleep sacks

Woolino + Nest Designs

Woolino covers spring through fall. For winter, long-sleeve warm ones from Nest. Fair warning: our house gets cold.

Sound machine

Marpac (Yogasleep) Dohm

Real fan-based white noise, no app, no subscription, no rainbow nightlight nobody asked for.

Monitor

Nanit

Clear picture, reliable app, no drama.

🌶️ Hot take The Hatch sounds awful. Tinny, digital, looping. My kids slept noticeably better once we switched to the Marpac's actual moving-air white noise. I said what I said.
💡 On the Owlet: I loved ours, but context matters. Both my boys have asthma and we've had hospital visits for low oxygen, so the Owlet sock gives me real peace of mind. The trick: we only use it when they're sick. As an every-night thing it's a false-alarm anxiety machine; as a sick-night tool it's brilliant. One more thing: I asked a pediatric ER doc about the sock on one of our visits, and they said it can sometimes read lower than baby's actual O2. Still, it's always safer to get checked than to be sorry.
🍼 Pacifiers: we tried them all. Both boys said no thank you. Buy one or two brands, not a drawer full — your baby is the one deciding this, not the reviews.

Feeding

Bottles

Start with MAM

Bottles are extremely baby-dependent. Do not stock up on any brand before your baby has voted. MAM is the best first guess IMO.

High chair

Stokke Tripp Trapp

We have two. I'm sorry, I just don't like any others as much. It grows with the kid and doesn't look like plastic playground equipment in your kitchen.

Travel high chair

Inglesina Fast

Clamps onto tables. Comes on every trip with us.

Nursing pillow

My Brest Friend or Boppy

Try a few — this is another fit-dependent one. I liked both of these.

🍼 The pump advice nobody gives you: rent a hospital-grade pump for the first few weeks instead of buying. Only buy your own if you actually plan to keep pumping. For what it's worth, I don't recommend exclusively pumping; it is soul-draining. Whatever you choose, don't let a $400 pump you never use judge you from the closet.

Chaos, but make it nutritious

Starting Solids

You need less than the baby-food industrial complex wants you to believe. Here's the short list.

1

Silicone pouch spouts: they prevent both spillage and the dreaded full-pouch squeeze onto your floor.

2

Stainless steel plates, bowls, cups & cutlery: unbreakable, dishwasher-safe, no plastic.

3

Munchkin weighted straw cup for water. Once they hit preschool, graduate to a Thermos Funtainer.

Yes, I'm embarrassingly crunchy about microplastics. The Munchkin cup gets a pass. Everyone has a price.
🧃 Permission to be lazy: feeding pouches are obviously not for meals, but they are an easy, sort-of-healthy snack on the go. Zero guilt here.

The glamour section

Diapering

Diapers & wipes

Costco. That's it.

Kirkland diapers and wipes. Save the fancy-diaper money for literally anything else.

Diaper cream

Rocky Mountain Soap Co. Bum Balm + Calmoseptine

Bum Balm for everyday mild irritation. When it's bad, Calmoseptine is the heavy artillery.

Changing pad

A cheap soft one + a few covers

We had the fancy wipeable Keekaroo and both boys hated it; it's always cold. Soft pad, washable covers, done.

Diaper pail

Munchkin — yes, get one

Pro tip: only poopy diapers go in the pail. Pee diapers go in the regular trash. Your refill budget will thank you.

🌶️ Hot take You don't need a diaper bag. Get a big cute pouch (Pehr and Baggu make great ones) with a few diapers, wipes, and hand sanitizer, and throw it in whatever bag you're already carrying. The $200 diaper backpack is a costume.
🧴 Bonus cleaning rec: Force of Nature. Slightly annoying that you have to "make" it every two weeks, but it kills germs, it's safe around babies and dogs, and it's great sprayed on the couch for dog odors. Worth it for the peace of mind alone.

Keep it simple

Clothes

Sleepers

Old Navy rib double-zip sleepers

My favorites, full stop. Double zips mean you're not fighting snaps at 3am. Buy them in every size.

Shoes

NO BABY SHOES.

Complete waste of money until they're walking. Once they walk, take them into a store to be fitted. Kid shoe sizing online is impossible. Calgary friends: we love Two Giraffes in Willow Park.

Things people will tell you that you need. You do not need them.

🛑

Don't

Full-size UppaBaby Vista/Cruz. Huge, heavy, and inconvenient, especially anywhere with real winters. The Minu is the one to get.

🛒

Don't

The "nursing cart." Never used mine once. It's a $60 way to store snacks you'll eat on the couch anyway.

👟

Don't

Baby shoes. Pure decoration until they walk. Then get fitted in-store; kid sizing is impossible online.

🎒

Don't

A dedicated diaper bag. A big cute pouch in your regular bag does the same job for a tenth of the price.

🧸

Don't

A mountain of toys. Less is more. See: spatula.

🧴

Don't

Wipeable "luxury" changing pads. They're cold and babies hate them. Cheap soft pad + covers.

You're on the registry too

For Mom 💛

The stuff nobody puts on the registry but everyone needs in week one.

1

Lots of maxi pads — no wings. Stock up before your due date. More than you think.

2

Frida Mom peri bottle. The upside-down angled one. The hospital one is not the same and we both know it.

3

Advil and Tylenol. Have both at home before you deliver. Ask your care team about alternating them.

4

Make padsicles before your due date. Witch hazel and aloe on a pad, wrap it up, freeze. Stock the freezer with a batch; future you will be so grateful.

5

Love Steady bloomers. These were so amazing postpartum. Designed by a physical therapist, they use flexible compression to support your core and pelvic floor: it feels like they're helping your organs settle back into place.
